You have infrastructure that costs too much, a pipeline nobody trusts, a platform that slows your team down, or a service nobody has written yet. I build and I fix, and I leave behind something your engineers can own.

## 01 What I help with

- **Cloud cost.** Multi-account consolidation, right-sizing, spot and autoscaling, egress. I have cut AWS bills 30-50% without touching the product roadmap.
- **GitOps delivery.** ArgoCD, Helm, Terraform. Declarative, drift-detected, no more deploys from someone's laptop.
- **CI/CD that stops rotting.** Reusable templates, automatic project detection, autoscaling runners. A pipeline is a product, not copy-pasted YAML.
- **Test infrastructure.** Quality gates inside the pipeline, contract tests between services, suites that run in parallel instead of overnight.
- **Backend services.** APIs, internal tools, data pipelines, CLIs, when what you need is software written rather than infrastructure fixed.

## 02 How I think

- Cloud cost is an architecture problem. The bill is a symptom.
- A pipeline is a product, and your engineers are its users.
- Quality gates belong in the pipeline, not in a checklist after it.
- If it is not in code, it does not exist.
- Optimize for the team that maintains it after I leave.

## 03 How we can work together

- **Cloud cost audit.** Two weeks, fixed scope. I map spend to architecture and hand you a prioritized list with effort and saving per item.
- **Project build.** Four to eight weeks, fixed scope. Platform migration, GitOps rollout, CI/CD rebuild, or test infrastructure.
- **Ongoing ownership.** Monthly retainer. I own platform and delivery so you do not need a full-time platform hire yet.
